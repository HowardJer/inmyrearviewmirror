ON THE SHOULDERS OF GIANTS

My wife has a Schwinn adjacent bicycle.
It is similar to a Schwinn without the brand name price tag.
in my youth, in our working class neighbourhood, there were a few Schwinn's.
But mostly the Schwinn adjacent CCM.
And for a lot of us the Schwinn adjacent hand me down from older brothers.

In Europe and Asia bicycles were are necessary form of transportion for the working class.
But in North Amarica, mainly Canada and the USA even in wrking class neighbourhoods automobiles were the main mode.
Except for us children.
To us, it was freedom.  
Bicycles gave the freedom to go where we wanted.
The corner of the block was no longer a limt.
With our bicycles we went beyond those limits.
The bicycle was a vehicle to explore the world.
with it we could cross the oceans like columbus and Magellan.
Or, thanks to Star Trek, the spce race and the Outer Limits we could fly into space.
With our bicycles we could travel to other lands, exotic locations and even distant planets.
We could flick on our turbo (a baseball card canchored to our forks with closthespins)

Uor bikes were freedom personified. Powered by the exuberance of youth and fueled by imagination;
we coud sail the seven seeas, portage between rivers an lakes, trek across endless deserts and even traverse the void between the tars....
A long as we were home in time for dinner.
